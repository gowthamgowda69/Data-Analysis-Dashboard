# SuperStore-Sales-Insights-Dashboard (Interactive Dashboard creation using MS Excel)
A dark-themed Super Store Sales Dashboard visualizing key performance indicators, multi-year seasonal trends (2014-2017), regional filters, and deep-dive breakdowns across product sub-categories, consumer segments, and shipping methods.
- <a href="https://github.com/gowthamgowda69/Data-Analysis-Dashboard/blob/main/Sample%20-%20Superstore.xlsx">Dataset</a>

# Questions (KPIs)

### 📈 Sales & Profit
* Why do sales and profits always spike in March?
* What causes the sharp revenue drop every April?
* Is a 15% net profit margin ($108K profit vs. $725K sales) healthy for this model?

### 📦 Products
* Do top sellers like Chairs ($102K) and Phones ($99K) actually bring in the highest profit margins?
* Are high-revenue Tables ($85K) profitable, or are they dragging down returns?
* Which macro category (Furniture vs. Technology) yields the best ROI?

### 🚚 Shipping & Customers
* Why is Standard Class shipping preferred so heavily over other methods?
* Can we optimize the supply chain to boost low "Same Day" shipping adoption?
* Which customer segment (Consumer vs. Corporate) shows the most consistent growth?

# Project Insight

* **Total Pocket Money (Sales):** $725.46K total sales made by the store.
* **Total Piggy Bank Savings (Profit):** $108.42K kept as pure profit.
* **Best Toy Choices (Top Products):** Chairs ($102K) and Phones ($99K) are the most popular items bought.
* **Favorite Delivery Choice (Shipping):** Standard Class is the clear favorite, moving 0.16M items.
* **The Big March Boom:** March is always the biggest month for making money, while April always sees a big drop.

# Conclusion

This **Super Store Sales Dashboard** successfully transforms raw transactional data into strategic business intelligence. By highlighting critical seasonal trends—such as the recurring March peak—and isolating top revenue drivers like Chairs and Phones, the dashboard provides a clear blueprint for optimizing inventory, targeting key customer segments, and refining regional shipping logistics to maximize overall profitability.
